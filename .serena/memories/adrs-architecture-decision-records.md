# Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)

**Created**: 2026-01-10
**Category**: Architecture Documentation

## Overview

An ADR captures a single Architectural Decision and its rationale, including trade-offs and consequences.

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **AD** | Justified design choice addressing functional/non-functional requirements |
| **ADR** | Document capturing one AD and its rationale |
| **ASR** | Architecturally Significant Requirement with measurable impact |

## Structure (Nygard Template)

1. **Title**: Short noun phrase (e.g., "ADR 1: Use React for UI")
2. **Status**: Proposed, Accepted, Deprecated, Superseded
3. **Context**: Forces at play, why decision needed
4. **Decision**: Chosen option
5. **Consequences**: Positive and negative outcomes

## Benefits

- Document decision rationale and trade-offs
- Create organizational decision logs
- Support agile and iterative processes
- Enable architectural knowledge management
- Establish common vocabulary

## Adoption

- AWS Prescriptive Guidance recommends ADRs
- Azure Well-Architected Framework features ADR guidance
- Part of Open Practice Library
